Our History

In 1929, Nigerian Electrical Supply Corporation Limited (London) was established to supply electricity to the mining industry in Plateau province, Nigeria. 1935 Indenture from the Nigerian Colonial authorities was granted to Nigerian Electrical Supply Corporation Limited, London to enable the development and operation of a hydro electrical power scheme in the border of the Plateau and Kaduna province. In 1969, Nigerian Electrical Supply Corporation (Nigeria) Limited (“NESCO”) a wholly owned subsidiary of the Nigerian Electrical Supply Corporation Limited (London) was established to take over the business of Nigerian Electrical Supply Corporation Limited (London) to operate the various hydro electrical power plants developed and continue the generation, distribution and transmission of electricity in the Plateau, Nasarawa and Kaduna provinces of Nigeria with effect from 18 November 1968 pursuant to Section 369(1) of the Companies Decree 1968. By 1972, 20 % of the share capital of NESCO sold to Benue Plateau State. Between 1978-79, various divestments of shares by the Nigerian Electrical Supply Corporation (London) were made to Plateau Investment Company, Benue State, the National Insurance Corporation of Nigeria (now known as NICON Insurance Corporation) and the Nigerian Mining Corporation in order to comply with Nigerian legislation requiring Nigerian companies to be majority held by Nigerian interests. During the same period and subsequently there have been various transfers of shares to wholly owned Nigerian state investment companies including the Benue Investment Company and the Nasarawa Investment Company as these states were created/determined their investment structures. 1993/94, there was a management buyout of the Nigerian assets of Nigerian Electrical Supply Corporation (London) through a 100% owned investment company, NESCO Overseas Holdings Limited (a company incorporated in England and Wales with company number 1604981). The 1998 deregulation of the Electricity industry in Nigeria removed the state power company NEPA’s monopoly and allowed private sector investment. Prior to deregulation the government electricity company NEPA had maintained a full monopoly of the electrical industry in Nigeria.

Completed Projects

Over our long years of experience, we have helped governments to roll out electricity networks. This diversification includes consultancy, building transmission and substation installations for governments and private owners. During emergencies or planned production breaks when your equipment or processes fail, NESCO is there to provide instant technical support and repairs. When you need spares and consumables, we guarantee to supply you with exactly the right spare part in full working order, within the agreed timeframe. High-voltage substations, the node points of today’s complex power transmission infrastructure, play a key role in meeting this demand safely and reliably. NESCO undertakes the construction of customized, state-of-the-art high-voltage substations. Our scope of supply and installation comprises much more than the high-voltage equipment that is required for the operation of a substation. It includes high- and medium-voltage switchgear, transformers, and equipment as well as all ancillary systems for control, protection, communication, and condition monitoring. Our services include operation and maintenance, repair and retrofit, final disposal and more. Additionally, we can help you proactively care for your equipment, providing failure-free operations, reducing costs for downtime or other non-productive periods and extending useful life. • Doruwa Babuje – Shendam – Lafia over 200km 33kV
• Jekko-Wamba-Akwanga-Doma-Keffi – Nassarawa Toto over 250km 33kV

Our Vision

The company is expanding its operations to enable it take over power distribution for whole Plateau, Nasarawa, Benue, Bauchi, Gombe and Adamawa States. Generating capacity would increase from the Company’s current hydro capacity of 26W to over 250mW and it is anticipated that additional hydro power could be generated as follows:

Plateau 15mW
Nasarawa 40mW
Lower Benue 70mW
Katsina 125mW.

Hydrological studies have been carried out for the Sha Falls (50mW). The Company is currently considering whether to proceed with this opportunity, along with the accelerated modernisation of the Company’s existing plants. Purchasing new greenfield plants at different locations and the construction of additional dams together with the procurement of additional distribution transformers and equipment to enable upgrading and rehabilitate substations and increase sales are distinct possibilities in the nearest future.
